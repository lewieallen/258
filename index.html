About Me



I am pretty well rounded in the world of digital. My skills lean heavily towards the web, with languages such as HTML/CSS, Javascript and PHP under my belt. I prefer to keep work strictly front-end, but I'm willing to go under the hood in certain circumstances. My passions are in front-end design and early stage product development. I have 2 years experience in teaching big companies how to leverage digital to their advantage.



I pride myself on high quality, mobile responsive, professional looking web pages as well as web apps. I also advise entreprenuers, SME's and small businesses in digital strategy and early stage prototyping, teaching them how to run a digital business/strategy, and make the most impact with few resources. I often talk at digital conferences and seminars too, highlighting subjects such as digital disruption and diversity in the technology industry.



I have recently started to design my own brand of laptop bags, with an aim to serve the new generation of digital workers, who carry less and do more. With a minimalist approach and an emphasis on functionality with great design, 258 is my first sole venture as a digital entreprenuer.

The "Build a Bank" Project

As part of a talk done by Gi Fernando(Freeformers Owner & Co-Founder) at the 2014 Barclays Digital Conference, I was asked to build a peer-to-peer lending app in a few hours to highlight the ease of making digital products today, as well as a warning to the financial industry that digital disruption can come from anywhere, not just their competitors. Built using Bootstrap v3, Facebook Login API and GoCardless API. See it here.

  • Client: Freeformers
  • Date: November 2014
  • Service: App Development (and a little bit of Acting)
